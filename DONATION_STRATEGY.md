## Revenue Model

### Platform Fee Structure:

#### Option A: Percentage Fee (Recommended)
- **5% platform fee** on all donations
- Lower than investment fee (2%)
- Covers payment processing + platform costs
- Industry standard for donation platforms

**Example:**
- $100 donation
- $5 goes to platform
- $95 goes to project
- Donor sees: "$100 donation ($95 to project, $5 platform fee)"

#### Option B: Optional Tip
- Donor chooses to add tip for platform
- Suggested: 10%, 15%, 20%, Custom
- 100% of donation goes to project
- Platform funded by tips

**Example:**
- $100 donation to project
- Donor adds 15% tip ($15)
- Total charge: $115
- Project gets: $100
- Platform gets: $15

#### Option C: Hybrid (Best Approach) ğŸ¯
- **3% platform fee** (mandatory)
- **Optional tip** (suggested 10%)
- Transparent breakdown

**Example:**
- $100 donation
- $3 platform fee (mandatory)
- $10 optional tip (donor choice)
- Total: $113
- Project gets: $97
- Platform gets: $13

## Legal & Compliance

### Important Distinctions:

**Donations vs Investments:**
- âœ… Donations: No equity, no returns, gift
- âœ… Investments: Equity stake, expect returns, securities

**Tax Implications:**
- Donations may be tax-deductible (if to non-profit)
- Platform must provide receipts
- Consult tax professional

**Regulations:**
- Less regulated than investments
- Still need clear terms
- Transparent fee disclosure
- Refund policy

### Required Disclosures:
```
"Donations are gifts and do not entitle you to 
equity, returns, or repayment. Donations support 
project development but do not guarantee project 
success. Platform fee: 3% + optional tip. 
Tax-deductibility depends on project status."
```

## Recommended Approach

### Start With:

1. **3% Platform Fee** (mandatory)
   - Transparent and fair
   - Covers costs
   - Lower than competitors

2. **Optional 10% Tip** (suggested)

   - Feels generous

   - Psychological pricing

   - Low cost to deliver
   - High perceived value
   - Builds loyalty

   - Predictable revenue
